Location

Why
Sighnaghi?

From an investor perspective, Signaghi offers attractive residential fundamentals:

  • Limited supply of organized apartment housing
  • Demand driven primarily by permanent residents
  • Low risk of large-scale speculative oversupply
  • Competitive construction economics
  • Favorable absorption for well-priced residential units

These characteristics support a conservative, execution-focused development strategy.

Execution

Development
Phasing

Aurra Residences will be developed through a block-by-block construction approach, comprising three residential blocks of approximately 5,000 sqm each, delivering 300+ Apartments across the full project.

Each block represents a separate development phase and is executed sequentially.

Housing Demand & Supply

Estimated Housing Demand vs Supply in Kakheti Region

The chart illustrates a significant gap between housing demand and modern supply in the Kakheti region. Out of approximately 60,000 households, nearly 70% of existing homes are in poor or outdated condition, creating strong replacement demand.

The long-term potential demand over the next 5 years is expected to exceed 15,000 units. Demand for new housing units in the Signaghi region is pegged at 900 units. The availability of new units in Kakheti region is below 500 units. This imbalance supports the case for new, well-planned residential developments in the Kakheti region.

Target Client Segments

Young Professionals & Working Families (60%)

A large portion of our target buyers comprises young, working individuals and families currently living with parents or in outdated housing. These are aspirational buyers seeking affordable yet modern homes to begin independent living.

Lifestyle Upgraders Seeking Better Amenities (20%)

This emerging segment includes middle-income residents and families seeking to upgrade from older, congested housing into modern, thoughtfully designed apartments.

Retirees and Empty Nesters (10%)

Retired professionals and older couples looking for serene and secure living with essential amenities and low maintenance.

Real Estate Investors Seeking Rental Income (10%)

Buyers aiming for long-term rental yield from tourism-driven tenants and seasonal demand.

Economics

Project Economics

  • Revenue generated through phased residential apartment sales
  • Phase-linked pricing enables early capital appreciation
  • Financial assumptions based on conservative absorption risk
  • Shared infrastructure efficiencies improve execution risk
  • Phase 1 requires the highest capital, with later phases being sales-funded
  • Sales-led funding reduces reliance on external capital and improves internal efficiencies
  • Returns improve across phases through pricing appreciation
  • Economics structured for early capital protection and later margin expansion
